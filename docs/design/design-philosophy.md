# Re_Call Design Philosophy

This is a constraint doc. Every future build references it. It pairs with the [Personalization System Doctrine](../product/personalization-system-doctrine.md) and the [Shop / Ship / Undertow product loop](../product/shop-ship-undertow.md).

## Core

Beauty over efficiency. Doing more of what you love is what makes you valuable, so the app should feel like something you *want* to open, not a tool you are obligated to use.

```text
The line between "fun" and "work" is artificial.
Re_Call's genius is erasing it.
Bring art to the reminder.
```

A productivity app should feel like self-discovery you would choose for pleasure. Every decision below serves that.

## Principles

### 1. Give, never ask

The first screen offers beautiful things to *choose from*. Never an empty state, never tiny grey effort labels, never a blank "generate from nothing" prompt.

- **Anti-models:** Apple Reminders' void, the blank chat cursor.
- **Models:** Shazam's single orb, Netflix "Choose Your Profile," Apple Weather's varied data tiles.

The user's first act is a selection, not a labor.

### 2. Templates are seeds, not answers

Value lives in personalization: uniqueness curated by the user, seeded from templates that *demonstrate* what curated looks like.

```text
Target reaction:
"I like these, but I have a better version for my life."
```

Success is the user going in and improving them — rearrange, hide, delete, rename. Editing is the product.

### 3. Numbers can lead; words never

Words are never the focal point. A number can be. No large text — type recedes, data carries the eye.

### 4. Eclectic, imperfect masonry — never a grid

Variable widths *and* lengths. Size implies importance and relationship. Strict alignment reads "corporate overlord, leave your true self at the door." Slight imperfection creates the involuntary urge to curate.

### 5. Figure/ground as living tension

Cézanne plus the Hoyt Sherman flash-room: an active ground, forms emerging from dark, the whole grasped in one glance.

Hero discipline is **Reduce + Anchor**:

```text
one focal point
one CTA
one anchor
delete anything that does not serve the action
```

### 6. Realism only

Actual detailed photography (Notion / Unsplash style) — not impressionism, not AI painterly, not cosmic. Covers live everywhere (page, per-card, full-screen); the message stays but turns translucent over the photo. AI removes the licensing wall that used to make this impractical.

### 7. Beautiful simplicity in color

A tight system: two blues, a neutral, and gold reserved for the *single* CTA. One accent, one contrast pair, everything else recedes.

```text
lapis            #286498
cerulean         #5E99BD
black-coral      #555A6B
american-silver  #C9CFD3
satin-gold       #C49E30   (CTA only)
```

### 8. Behavioral science through structure, not copy

Nudges live in the layout, never in coaching text:

- Incompleteness / Zeigarnik — open slots that want filling.
- Size-as-importance.
- Similarity and proximity grouping.
- Variable reward.

### 9. Aptitude-fit (Adam's profile)

- **Non-spatial thinker:** no 3D metaphors, no unlabeled node webs, no blank canvases.
- **Fast numerical/visual scanner:** varied scannable tiles plus numbers.
- Lead with what things *do*, minimal text.
- One decision per screen.
- Labeled choices.
- Visible proof.

## Through-Line

The aesthetic serves the doctrine:

```text
memory is narrative; life is relational
reminders go deeper, not broader
you build the rules; AI helps with the language
```

The look and feel is what makes a productivity app feel like self-discovery you would choose for pleasure. Beauty is not decoration here — it is the mechanism that turns obligation into desire.
